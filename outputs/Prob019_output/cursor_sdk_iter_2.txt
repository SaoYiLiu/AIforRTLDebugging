## Rationale
- **Suspected bug (L035):** The difference output uses `~(Y1_n & Y2_n | Y4_n & Y7_n)`, which ORs two partial AND terms instead of combining all four minterms that make `D=1`.
- **Expected full-subtractor behavior:** With decoder address `{A,B,Ci}`, `D=1` for minterms 1, 2, 4, and 7 (001, 010, 100, 111) — i.e. when any of `Y1_n`, `Y2_n`, `Y4_n`, or `Y7_n` is active-low.
- **Why the buggy expression fails:** For input 001 (`A=0,B=0,Ci=1`), only `Y1_n=0`; `Y1_n & Y2_n` is 0, but `Y4_n & Y7_n` is 1, so the OR is 1 and `D = ~1 = 0`. That wrongly forces `D` low whenever minterms 4 and 7 are both inactive-high, even when minterm 1 is selected.
- **Waveform evidence:** `D_dut` stays 0 for the whole run while `D_ref` toggles (first mismatch at 20 ps when `Ci` rises with `A=B=0`). `Co_dut` matches `Co_ref` throughout — consistent with `Co` (L036) being correct and only `D` (L035) being wrong.
- **Structured feedback:** 48/57 mismatches, all on `D`; `Co` has zero mismatches — same single-line diagnosis.
- **Fix applied:** Change L035 to `assign D = ~(Y1_n & Y2_n & Y4_n & Y7_n);`, matching the reference and the same NAND-of-active-low-minterms pattern already used correctly for `Co` on L036. Simulation passes with 0/57 mismatches.
