## Rationale
- **Second-stage prefix tree is broken (lines 21–24).** Every `G2`/`P2` entry is computed from `G1[0]` and `P1[0]` instead of the corresponding bit pair. A Brent-Kung black cell at index `i/2` must combine bits `i` and `i+1`: `G2[i/2] = G1[i+1] | (P1[i+1] & G1[i])` and `P2[i/2] = P1[i+1] & P1[i]`. This corrupts all upstream `G3`–`G6`/`P3`–`P6` values and the final carry `C[32]`.
- **Harness evidence for carry_out (Test Case 2).** With `a = b = 0x7FFFFFFF` and `carry_in = 0`, the sum is correct (`FFFFFFFE`) but `carry_out` is `1` instead of `0`. MSB is `0+0+carry_from_bit_30 = 1` with no overflow, so `C[32]` must be 0. The flattened second stage makes every `G2[k]=1`, inflating prefix generates and falsely asserting `carry_out`.
- **Sum uses OR instead of XOR (line 87).** Bit `i` of the result is `P1[i] ^ C[i]` (with `C[0]` replaced by `carry_in`). `assign sum = P1 | {C[31:1], carry_in}` forces bits high wherever either propagate or carry is 1, which explains wrong sums such as Test Case 7 (`EEAEEAEF` vs `EEEEEEEF`), Test Case 9 (`FFFFFFFF` vs `9999999A`), and Test Case 11 (`77777777` vs `33333334`).
- **Third through sixth prefix stages (lines 27–49) and carry fanout (lines 51–85) follow the correct Brent-Kung pairing pattern** once `G2`/`P2` are fixed; no changes needed there.
- **Fix is minimal:** correct the second-stage generate indices and change the sum expression to XOR.
