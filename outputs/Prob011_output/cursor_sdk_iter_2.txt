## Rationale
- **Suspected bug (L011):** The generate term `G` is computed with XOR (`A_in^B_in`) instead of AND. Per the carry look-ahead spec, `G_i = A_i AND B_i`, while `P_i = A_i XOR B_i` (correct on L010).
- **Why this breaks the adder:** `G_i` indicates a carry generated at bit *i* when both inputs are 1. Using XOR makes `G` identical to `P`, so carries are never generated correctly and the carry chain collapses.
- **Waveform evidence at first failure (t=10 ps):** At t=5, `A_in=1`, `B_in=1`, `C_1=0`. Reference `S_ref=10` (binary `2`). With the bug, `G[0]=0` instead of `1`, so `C[0]=0` and `S_dut` diverges immediately; `tb_mismatch` asserts at t=5.
- **Structured feedback:** 398/425 mismatches, first at 10 ps; 368 mismatches on `S` and 226 on `CO` — consistent with a wrong generate signal affecting both sum and carry-out.
- **Causal trace:** Inputs change at t=5; mismatch follows on the next sample boundary. No issues in the carry (L013), sum (L014), or `CO` (L015) expressions themselves.
- **Fix applied:** Change L011 from `assign G=A_in^B_in;` to `assign G=A_in&B_in;`. Verified: 0 mismatches in 425 samples against the reference testbench.

## FixedTopModule
```verilog
module TopModule(
    input       [3:0]       A_in  ,
    input       [3:0]       B_in  ,
    input                   C_1   ,
  
    output   wire           CO    ,
    output   wire [3:0]     S
);
    wire [3:0] G,P,C;
    assign P=A_in^B_in;
    assign G=A_in&B_in;
     
    assign C={G[3]|(P[3]&C[2]), G[2]|(P[2]&C[1]), G[1]|(P[1]&C[0]), G[0]|(P[0]&C_1)};
    assign S={P[3]^C[2], P[2]^C[1], P[1]^C[0], P[0]^C_1};
    assign CO=C[3];
     
endmodule
```
